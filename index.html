About the Lab

Our research is dedicated to pioneering intelligent technological solutions—harnessing Artificial Intelligence (AI), Machine Learning (ML), Deep Learning, Wearable Sensors, IoT, and Remote Sensing—to tackle complex, data-driven challenges across three primary, interconnected domains: Health, Environment, and Security. The core of our work involves a data-driven and objective approach to assess, monitor, and improve outcomes. Our foundational expertise lies in Health-Tech, where we develop AI models, particularly with wearable sensors, for the objective assessment and monitoring of individuals. This includes a strong focus on neurological disorders (such as Parkinson’s disease and post-stroke rehabilitation), elderly care, and advanced systems for cardiovascular activity monitoring. Crucially, our lab has expanded its scope into two critical areas: Environmental and Disaster Technology: We apply advanced AI, remote sensing, and even LLM-based reasoning to address global challenges, including cloudburst and flood prediction and the tech-driven combating of deforestation. Forensic & Creative Technologies: We leverage our expertise in multimodal data analytics and AI/ML to undertake joint research in digital evidence analysis, multimedia technology, and creative computing for forensic and security applications. By maintaining a core focus on intelligent systems while integrating these diverse application fields, our research strives to translate cutting-edge computational methods into high-impact, real-world solutions.

Core Research Areas

My expertise is concentrated in the following interdisciplinary domains:

  • Expertise in wearable sensors, IMUs, and physiological monitoring for non-invasive assessment of human motion, sleep, and cardiovascular activity.
  • Developed AI-driven diagnostic models for Parkinson’s disease and post-stroke motor assessment using time-series physiological data.
  • Experience in large-scale multi-sensor data acquisition and processing in real-world clinical and ambient settings (e.g., iCARE-IT, DAPHNE, and E-Motions projects).
  • Modeling of cerebellar microcircuits, synaptic plasticity, and neural dynamics to simulate brain-inspired control mechanisms.
  • Application of spiking neural models and biologically realistic simulations (EBrains 2.0, CNS 2025).
  • Integration of neuroscience principles into adaptive robotic and AI systems for motor learning and sensory fusion.
  • Expertise in CNNs, RNNs, and LSTMs for image, time-series, and biomedical data analysis.
  • Hands-on experience with YOLO, VGGFace2, FaceNet, and transformer-based architectures for detection and recognition tasks.
  • Certified in Machine Learning for Time-Series Data (Python) and AI applications in health, surveillance, and robotics domains.
  • Proficient in Python, R, and MATLAB for data-driven modeling, statistical learning, and visualization.
  • Applied data mining and predictive analytics for healthcare, environmental monitoring, and security applications.
  • Publications in Frontiers in Big Data and Computational Intelligence and Neuroscience demonstrating interdisciplinary analytical approaches.
  • Design and implementation of AI- and IoT-enabled rehabilitation, assistive, and monitoring systems for elderly and neurodegenerative patients.
  • Collaboration in European projects such as ACCRA, KERAAL, and DAPHNE focusing on robot-mediated physical therapy and smart health ecosystems.
  • Emphasis on ethical, privacy-preserving, and human-in-the-loop AI for sustainable healthcare innovation.
  • Development of distributed AI pipelines for real-time video analytics and facial recognition across multi-camera Safe City infrastructures.
  • Implementation of edge-based processing for latency reduction and enhanced synchronization across IoT devices.
  • Integration of computer vision, network optimization, and temporal alignment strategies for scalable public safety solutions.
